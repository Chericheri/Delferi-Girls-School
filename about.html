Our Story

Empowering young women through technology education since 2010

Our Mission

To bridge the gender gap in technology by providing accessible, high-quality computer education that equips young women with technical skills, professional confidence, and career opportunities in Kenya's growing digital economy.

Our Vision

To become East Africa's leading institution for women's technology education, producing graduates who transform industries and inspire the next generation of female tech leaders.

Our Journey

From humble beginnings to national recognition

2010

Foundation

Founded by Dr. Wanjiku Mwangi in a small Nairobi office with just 12 students and 2 instructors, offering basic computer literacy courses.

2013

First Expansion

Moved to our current Kilimani campus and launched our first professional certification programs in web development and graphic design.

2016

Industry Partnerships

Established partnerships with major tech companies including Safaricom, Andela, and Microsoft for internships and curriculum development.

2019

National Recognition

Awarded "Best Technical Training Institution for Women" by the Kenyan Ministry of Education.

2023

Current Status

Now serving over 200 students annually with 15 full-time faculty members and 8 specialized programs.

Our Core Values

The principles that guide everything we do

Inclusive Excellence

We maintain high academic standards while ensuring accessibility for students from all backgrounds through scholarships and flexible programs.

Community Impact

We measure success not just by graduate employment rates, but by how our students use their skills to benefit their communities.

Innovative Teaching

We continuously evolve our curriculum to match industry needs, incorporating the latest technologies and teaching methodologies.

Safe Environment

We provide a harassment-free learning space where students can focus on growth without gender-based barriers.
